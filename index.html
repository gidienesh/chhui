What makes Chui Lodge special?

Lake Naivasha

Lake Naivasha, nestled within the Oserengoni Wildlife Sanctuary, beckons tourists seeking an immersive experience amidst abundant wildlife and stunning landscapes. With over 1500 hippos and 400 bird species, including the iconic African Fish Eagle, the lake offers a haven for wildlife enthusiasts and birdwatchers alike. Chui Lodge, a community-driven project within the sanctuary, provides eco-friendly accommodation with eight luxurious cottages, each offering unique views of the Rift Valley. From boat safaris to exploring the adjacent Lake Oloidien, guests can indulge in diverse outdoor activities. The lodge's commitment to sustainability extends to its cuisine, featuring organic ingredients sourced from its own farm. With professionally trained chefs, guests can savor delectable meals amidst the tranquil ambiance of the African bush. Chui Lodge, considered Naivasha's best-kept secret, offers a memorable retreat in the Rift Valley, complemented by its strategic location for exploring nearby attractions like Maasai Mara and Amboseli National Park. Accessible by a 2.5-hour drive or a short flight from Nairobi, Chui Lodge invites travelers to unwind and connect with nature in this sanctuary where wildlife thrives under the careful stewardship of its founders, Hans and June Zwager, and their son Peter

Oserian Two Lakes Experience with Chui Lodge

A lot of tourists traveling to Lake Naivasha don’t know there is a second volcanic crater lake next to Lake Naivasha, called Lake Oloidien. For a few years now, the two lakes are connected due to the high-water level. The name ‘Oloidien’ means salty. Lake Oloidien was originally a freshwater lake connected to Lake Naivasha, but in the late 1990s, it became very salty, probably due to the lack of outlets for the water. This increase in salinity led to the death and migration of many animals and birds. Despite this, the lake's high salt content was suitable for lesser flamingos, which made their home there until climate change caused the waters of Lake Oloidien and Lake Naivasha to mix, reducing the salinity of Oloidien and leading to the flamingos' disappearance. Although Flamingos disappeared, there is still a varied range of birds that can be observed when you visit the two lakes, like African fish eagles, cormorants, kingfishers, herons and pelicans. You will also find many hippos across the lake. The surrounding dead trees make it a picture-perfect place to visit. The best time to do a boat tour on the Oserian two lakes is in the early morning. Chui Lodge offers boat tours in a normal wooden boat or a speed boat.

Chui Lodge Amenities

Chui Lodge, nestled in the heart of Naivasha, offers a luxurious retreat amidst stunning landscapes and abundant wildlife. With eight spacious cottages, each boasting a private veranda with views of the Rift Valley escarpment and Wildlife Sanctuary, guests are invited to feel at home. Decorated with unique furnishings, including four-poster king-size olive wood beds, the cottages exude elegance and comfort. From cozy indoor fireplaces to well-appointed amenities like tea/coffee stations and mini-bars, every detail is designed for relaxation. Deluxe rooms, with their own lounge areas and additional sofa beds, provide a more expansive retreat. Named after local families, each cottage tells its own story, reflecting the lodge's rich heritage. Crafted from sustainable materials sourced within the sanctuary, Chui Lodge blends seamlessly with its surroundings, offering an authentic African experience. The lodge's main area and cottages are enveloped by lush greenery, creating a serene atmosphere for guests to unwind. Wildlife sightings are common, with buffalo, giraffes, zebras, and even leopards often spotted from the private patios. Guests can also enjoy a heated swimming pool overlooking the waterhole and sanctuary, providing a refreshing respite after safari adventures.
